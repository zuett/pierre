MONDRIAN'S MIRAGE

A portal through art

Mondrian's Mirage is an artistic endeavor that seeks to explore the interplay between the physical and the metaphysical, the concrete and the abstract. Inspired by the pioneering work of Piet Mondrian, a Dutch artist renowned for his abstract compositions, this project reimagines his iconic 1922 oil painting, "Composition with Blue, Red, Yellow, and Black." Mondrian's work, which epitomizes his revolutionary approach to art, is characterized by its use of primary colors, straight lines, and a clear separation of areas, all of which challenge traditional representations of reality and position him as a forerunner of abstract art. This project harnesses the capabilities of Unity 3D to translate Mondrian's two-dimensional painting into a three-dimensional space, effectively transforming it into an architectural form that users can navigate. As participants journey through this virtual environment, they are guided along a path that culminates in a room displaying the painting. Upon nearing the artwork, an immersive experience unfolds: viewers seemingly enter the painting itself, only to find themselves in freefall through its structure. This is achieved by cleverly teleporting the user to a vantage point above the architectural form, which mirrors the painting's layout and proportions from this new perspective. This dynamic shift from tangible to intangible underscores the project's thematic core, as it plays with the notion of proximity and interaction with art, allowing users to experience the painting in an entirely novel and unattainable way.
